Some Goals I Will Accomplish In Office:

  • Decreasing Screen Time

  • Computer Program

    • I have created a computer program that will take attendance for me. To plug in the information and figure out who is absent should take me no longer than 30 seconds, even with my slower computer. It will be easier to do on a google meet than a Zoom, so it might take only 20 seconds to do it on a google meet. However, that is much faster and much more efficent that calling out names. After I take the attendance, the teacher will confirm that the people who I said were absent were really absent and then go to pupilpath and fill out the sheet accordingly. Taking attendence for the teacher is not the only thing that I plan to do to reduce screen time.

  • Study Sheet

    • I will also make a study guide to reduce screen time. At first, one might think: "How can creating a study guide reduce screen time?" The answer to that is that not only will a study guide (with everything a student needs to know for their classes) help them study, but a study guide can also help someone complete their homework faster. This is because on the study guide, we will make sure to explain all the topics in a simple way so that if someone needs help with their homework, they can just go to the study guide to help them learn better. Also, creating a study guide can help a student study for their test. Instead of desperately looking for the material that the student needs to study, they can just go to the study guide, where we will create one page with everything they need to know for the test. The student could print out the one sheet, reducing screen time. However, a student wouldn't be able to print out every page of notes/teacher's slides that they need to know for a test. Therefore, creating a study guide can help reduce screen time.

  • Talking with Teachers

    • I will also talk with the teachers. Sometimes, a teacher could be doing something that they are not aware of, during class, that could be increasing our screen time. Talking to teachers can help us come up with realistic solutions that can help make class even 5 minutes shorter. If every class was 5 minutes shorter, our weekly screen time would be reduced by 2 1/2 hours.

  • Other Plans

    • I have other plans to reduce screen time, but you will have to elect me if you want to hear them. I know that screen time is a major issue for students. After a long day at the computer, my head hurts, and my eyes as well, so I understand these challenges. Also when students are sitting at the computer, they get little exercise. Even walking to the kitchen to get a snack between classes is better than sitting as long as you would on an airplane each day. Also, large amounts of screen time, which cause eye pain and discomfort, can hinder sleep, one of the most important things for a teenager to get (for learning and devolpment). Overall, anything we can do to reduce screen time would drastically help.

  • Helping Students Learn Better

My poitical party understands that it is extremly difficult to stare in front the the computer and stay focused and learn. So, we will have many strategies to make sure that the students actually understood and know the topic.

  • Creating a study sheet

    • I know that this was already talked about in the last section, but I want to talk about it a little more. On this website, if I become President, you will find all the information that you need to know for the tests. I will write down everything that we go over in class, and write it in language that we can all understand. Everytime you go to this website, you will get an alert at the top (just like the one when you went to this page) that will remind you of the tests coming up. I will also create a section called study sheets. If you would click on that, you would see links to google docs for each class. This can be extremly helpful, because if you have everything on one page, it will also be printable, reducing screen time. I might even create an app for studying, but that will come no sooner than March 30th.

  • Creating fun activites for you to learn from

    • By this I mean that I will encourage teachers to make kahoots and quizizz (or make them myself) to make learning fun and enjoyable. I will also try to have teachers put kids in breakout rooms more often (but you get to pick who you are with) so that we can also learn from our peers.

  • Ensuring Respect and Resolving Issues

What "Ensuring Respect and Resolving Issues" means is that I will create policies that will help bring more order to the classroom, and resolve issues. I will also, as the name of this section says, make sure that everyone is respecting and treating each other kindly. Also, if students come to me with an issue betweem them, I will do my best to resolve it, and if I can't, then I will reach out to an adult for help. I mentioned earlier in this paragraph that I would put some policies in place to keep respect and order in the classroom - here they are:

  • Speaking Policy

    • Many people when we are having a discussion love to participate - and that's good. But what's not good is when one person is preventing others from speaking because they are taking too long. As president, I want to make sure that everyone's voice can be heard, even in a classroom with 33 students and limited time. Therefore, I will come up with speaking policies to limit the amount of time someone can talk or respond to something. I will give more information about this during my presidency.

  • Participation Policy

    • Participation is a big problem for many teachers - they often stuggle trying to get their students to talk in class. My administration will come up with plans for participation where people can get extra credit for an abnomal amount of participation (in a good way, from like never pariticpating to a couple times a week). I will also increase the opportunities for participation so that everyone's voice can be heard, which my administration believes is very important. With that said, we will definitely not force any student to participate if they don't feel comfortable doing so. Instead, we will try to encourage participation with awards, not encourage them with punishments for not participating.
